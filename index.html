The man who revolutionized football

Johan Cruyff, the legendary Dutch footballer, wearing the Ajax Amsterdam shirt

Johan Cruyff's timeline:

  • 1947 - On April 25, Johan Cruyff is born in Amsterdam, Netherlands.
  • 1957 - Begins playing football at Ajax Amsterdam, the club where he would spend much of his career.
  • 1964 - Makes his debut in the Dutch league with Ajax.
  • 1971-1973 - Wins three consecutive Ballon d'Or awards.
  • 1973 - Transfers to FC Barcelona for a record fee at the time.
  • 1974 - Leads the Dutch national team to the World Cup final, where they would lose to Germany.
  • 1978-1979 - Leads the Dutch national team to the World Cup final, where they would lose to Germany
  • 1981-1983 - Plays for Levante in Spain and the Washington Diplomats in the United States before retiring from football.
  • 1985-1988 - Begins his coaching career at Ajax Amsterdam.
  • 1988 - Leads Ajax to win the UEFA Champions League.
  • 1992 - Leads FC Barcelona to win their first UEFA Champions League as a coach.
  • 1999-2011 - Works as a commentator and sports analyst on Dutch television.
  • 2016 - Passes away from lung cancer.
